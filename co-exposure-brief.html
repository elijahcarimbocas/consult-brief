Research-backed review of where recovery stands, building on the Day 19 flags. The reframe: what still limits mornings is most likely not more chamber time. The highest-value moves left are the sleep study (probable OSA), a clean thyroid redraw, and a formal neuropsych baseline. A self-directed interim stack is now in place while those are arranged.

Objective recovery trajectory

Single acute event, no loss of consciousness (the strongest positive prognostic sign), acute symptoms cleared within ~48 h, delayed head-heaviness resolved. Expected course: most CO-attributable fog clears over weeks 3 to 12 (roughly early August to mid-September), with a small tail improving toward one year.

Plateau test
CO recovery is a decay curve. If fog keeps dropping week over week, that is CO resolving on schedule. If it flattens into a morning-worst floor, the remainder is not CO, pointing to sleep, thyroid, or mood.
Objective baseline
Subjective clarity correlates weakly with measured cognition, and it is also the variable being used to judge treatment. A formal neuropsych evaluation (full battery, not a bedside screen) gives a real baseline to repeat at ~3 months. Referral from PCP or Dr. Dooley; a standard clinical neuropsychologist is sufficient, Barrow-tier not required.

Additional exposure · venous blood lead level

Rationale
Suppressed, over-gassed, indoor firing amplifies both CO and lead. Fine primer lead is highly bioavailable and unaffected by FMJ jackets, and suppressors redirect gas toward the shooter. Lead toxicity independently causes fog, memory trouble, and irritability, overlapping the CO picture.
Request
Add a venous blood lead level. Chronic burden is likely low (about 3 to 4 outdoor trips per year, no indoor habit), so this checks the single acute spike. If mildly elevated, treatment is source control plus time, not chelation.

Sleep workup and treatment options

The sleep study is the fork that decides whether the morning-worst fog has a second, HBOT-independent cause. Diagnosis comes before any treatment device.

Diagnosis
Physician-ordered home sleep study preferred (insurance-friendly). Direct-to-consumer FDA-cleared home tests (WatchPAT, Lofta, Daybreak) are a valid faster fallback. A negative or borderline home test with persistent symptoms warrants in-lab polysomnography, since home tests miss mild OSA. Wearables (Oura, Apple Watch, ring pulse oximeter) are screening only, not diagnostic.
If moderate to severe
CPAP is the gold standard and most effective option; an oral appliance is the fallback if CPAP is not tolerated.
If mild to moderate (esp. positional)
Oral appliance / mandibular advancement device (e.g. Daybreak) is well tolerated and effective, alongside positional therapy and weight loss. CPAP optional.
Decision rule
The AHI and severity choose the treatment. No treatment device purchased before the number.

Thyroid, selenium, and metabolic workup

The candidate is hypothyroidism (underactive), not hyperthyroidism. It fits a coherent cluster: cognitive fog, global emotional flatness, fatigue, and weight-loss resistance.

Weight-loss resistance
Held around 230 lb through the second half of 2025 despite heavy training (5 to 6 sessions/week at 5 AM, cleaned-up diet since Sep 2025, no alcohol, social dancing). Rose to the low 240s on the deliberate HBOT-window surplus, now trending down since dropping it.
Interpretation
A real hypothyroid data point, but confounded: body composition (muscular build, so the scale understates fat loss, track waist and body-fat rather than weight), calories (the plateau fits maintenance intake, and the scale clearly tracks intake given the surplus-driven rise and current drop), and sleep-apnea plus possible insulin resistance, both of which blunt fat loss.
Labs to draw (biotin held 2 to 3 days)
Comprehensive thyroid panel (ordered). Add a selenium level (the T4-to-T3 cofactor, status is part of the question) and A1c plus insulin (metabolic and insulin-resistance angle; berberine and gymnema in use).
Selenium timing
Stopped about a week ago. Hold it stable, do not restart, until after the thyroid draw, because selenium changes actual thyroid function (T4-to-T3), unlike biotin which only skews the assay. Resume a moderate dose (about 100 to 200 mcg/day) only if the level is low; do not megadose.
